Discover Lake Manyara with Precious Moments Safaris

Lake Manyara National Park provides a compact yet captivating safari experience with a region of 325 km^2. It also holds the distinction of being home to the world’s highest concentration of baboons. The best ecosystems for birdwatching are also available boasting over 2 million gry flamingos and over 400 other bird species. The reigning lion of the park, the tree climbing lions capture the hearts and eyes of those who visit the park with their sheer magnificiece alongside gargantuan herds of elephants that roam the land. Manyara Park’s position near the Ngorongoro Crater and Serengeti can be identified as a Jewel on the northern circuit of Tanzania paving way for Other Destinations.

TRUST IN EXPERIENCE

Why Visit Lake Manyara?

Unique Wildlife: The overwhelming presence of large mammals guarantees excellent game viewing. Spot the extraordinary tree-climbing lions, alongside elephants, giraffes, zebras, and enormous troops of baboons. Birdwatching Haven: Top destination for birders is between November to April due to over 400 bird species including flamingos, pelicans, and silver-cheeked hornbills. Migratory birds also arrive during this season. Diverse Ecosystems: Ideal for a half or full day excursion, the park’s 11 ecosystems offer abundant landscapes for exploration, which include dense forests, expansive plains, and soda lakes. Scenic Beauty: The lake’s shimmering waters and Rift Valley escarpment standing over 400 meters high create breathtaking views, perfect for those seeking stunning landscapes. Variety of Activities: Beside game drives, visit the cultural sights, enjoy canoeing, treetop walks, or night safaris for a fulfilling encounter. Accessibility: Due to its location near Arusha and relatively smaller size, it is a less busy alternative to Serengeti National Park, perfect for expanding safari routes.

FAQs – ABOUT LAKE MANYARA

When is the best time to visit Lake Manyara National Park?

The dry season (June to October) is ideal for wildlife viewing, as animals congregate around water sources, and vegetation is thinner. For birdwatching, the wet season (November to April) is best, with migratory birds and lush scenery. Avoid March and April for heavy rains, which may limit access.

How do I get to Lake Manyara National Park?

The park is a 126 km (2-hour) drive from Arusha on a good tarmac road. Alternatively, fly from Kilimanjaro International Airport to Lake Manyara Airstrip (40 minutes) or from Arusha (30 minutes). It’s easily accessible en route to Ngorongoro or Serengeti.

Is it worth going to Lake Manyara?

Among the most incredible destinations for wildlife spotting in Tanzania, this park is especially celebrated for its tree-climbing lions, elephant herds and birdwatching opportunities. Despite its relatively small size, Lake Manyara National Park is one of the best safari parks in Tanzania.

Are there accommodations within the park?

There are no lodges inside the park, but nearby options include Escarpment Luxury Lodge, Lake Manyara Serena Lodge, and Migombani Camp, offering stunning views and easy access. Two public campsites are available for self-sufficient campers, but facilities are basic.

Is Lake Manyara worth visiting compared to Serengeti or Ngorongoro?

While smaller than Serengeti or Ngorongoro, Lake Manyara offers a unique, less crowded experience with diverse habitats, tree-climbing lions, and excellent birdlife. It’s perfect as a day trip or a soft introduction to a safari, especially for those short on time.

WHAT TO DO

Top Activities in Lake Manyara National Park

Game Drives

The most popular activity, game drives offer sightings of tree-climbing lions, elephants, giraffes, and baboons. Morning drives catch active wildlife, while evening drives reveal nocturnal animals like hyenas. Night game drives, including permits) provide a rare chance to spot lions hunting or hippos grazing.

Birdwatching

With over 400 species, including flamingos, pelicans, and crowned eagles, the park is a birder’s delight. The wet season (November to April) is prime for migratory birds, and the lake’s edge is perfect for spotting waterbirds. Bring binoculars for the best experience.

Treetop Walkway

Tanzania’s first treetop walkway, an 18-meter-high, 1,312-foot suspension bridge, offers a bird’s-eye view of the forest. Spot monkeys, birds, and butterflies while guides share insights on flora and fauna.
